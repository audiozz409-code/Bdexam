Why Two Different Scales Exist

Bangladesh's SSC and HSC public exams use a 5.00-point GPA scale, while most universities report academic performance on a 4.00-point CGPA scale modeled on the UGC-style grading system. The two scales don't map with a single formula because their letter-grade boundaries differ — a university's B+ and B- grades, for example, have no direct SSC/HSC equivalent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between GPA and CGPA in Bangladesh?
GPA (out of 5.00) applies to SSC, HSC, and equivalents. CGPA (out of 4.00) is the standard university scale for semester and overall academic performance.
Is there an exact conversion formula?
No single universal formula exists — this tool maps by nearest equivalent letter grade, the standard reference method. Always confirm with your specific university's official table for formal use.
